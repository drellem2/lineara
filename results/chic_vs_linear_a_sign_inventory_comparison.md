## Reading

- The CHIC syllabographic subset has **96 distinct signs** (target population for chic-v5+ value extraction). Linear A has **69 distinct AB-syllabograms** observed in its corpus.
- The CHIC distribution is heavy-tailed: the top-10 most frequent CHIC syllabograms cover **49%** of all syllabographic occurrences, vs **36%** for Linear A's top-10.
- Top-29 CHIC signs cover 80% of syllabographic tokens; Linear A needs top-30 signs for the same coverage.

- **Distribution-shape verdict:** CHIC syllabographic frequency distribution is **heavier-tailed (more concentrated)** than Linear A's AB-syllabogram distribution.

Both corpora exhibit Zipfian behavior; the CHIC corpus is smaller but the per-sign frequency profile is in the same order-of-magnitude regime, which means downstream chic-v3+ harnesses can use the same heavy-tail-sensitive metrics that the Linear A pipeline uses.

## Caveats

- The CHIC corpus is ~2.0× smaller than the Linear A corpus by sign-token count (1420 vs 2894). Tail estimates (top-20+, percentile k for 80% coverage) are correspondingly noisier on the CHIC side. The comparison is a sanity-check, not a precise statistical claim.
- CHIC sign-class assignment is per the catalog numeric-range rule (see README); a future chic-v3 reclassification may shift signs in/out of the syllabographic subset and shift these counts.
- Linear A AB-tokens here include all AB-prefixed sign tokens; LOG-prefixed (logogram) tokens are excluded. Sign-class consistency between the two corpora is approximate.
