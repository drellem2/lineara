# Paired-difference leaderboard — sign_prediction_perplexity_v0

Generated by `scripts/paired_diff_rollup.py` (mg-ddee). For each substrate candidate, the matched control candidate is the one in the phonotactically-matched control pool that pins to the SAME (inscription, span). Among those, the closest phoneme sequence (by edit distance) wins. The substrate − control delta is the primary score. Substrate candidates without an exact-window control match are dropped from the aggregate.

**Acceptance gate (mg-ddee):** for at least one substrate pool, the per-surface paired_diff medians must have median > 0 with one-tail p < 0.05. The Wilcoxon signed-rank test (preferred for paired-difference statistics on per-surface medians) is the primary; the sign test is reported alongside as a distribution-free robustness check.

## Notes

- Total substrate candidates with an exact-window control match: **16723**.
- A `paired_diff` of +N means the substrate candidate scored N higher than its phonotactically-matched control. The leaderboard ranks substrate identity by the size of that gap, after phonotactic baseline is subtracted by construction.
- Wilcoxon W+ is the sum of positive ranks under the signed-rank transform; the one-tail p-value tests `median > 0`.
- Sign-test counts surfaces with positive paired-diff median; the one-tail p-value tests `P(median > 0) > 0.5` against a 0.5 null.
