## A. Project state snapshot (as of chic-v15 merge / 2026-05-06)

The Lineara product line operates under a **5-pillar discipline structure**:

1. **Per-surface coherence** — substrate-LM scoring at the inscription
   level; right-tail bayesian gates per pool.
2. **Per-inscription cascade-candidate analysis** — within-inscription
   consistency of mechanical readings against scholar-proposed values
   and within-corpus context.
3. **Scholar comparison** — mechanical-vs-scholar agreement on a 35-entry
   reading set (LA-side) plus chic-v2 paleographic anchors (CHIC-side).
4. **Within-window context inspection** — per-candidate structural
   consistency against anchored neighbours and canonical formulas.
   Promoted to load-bearing fourth pillar in v30 (mg-ee1f) following
   chic-v14's anti-evidentiary verdict on cross-pool L3 alone.
5. **LOO held-out validation** — leave-one-out recovery of known
   anchors as the discipline-protecting fifth pillar (chic-v9, v28,
   chic-v14).

## B. Supportable claims (post-chic-v15)

These are the claims the framework supports as of the commit at which
this handoff is filed. They are unchanged from v30 except for the
chic-v15-derived refinement on cross-pool L3.

### Population-level substrate-LM-phonotactic kinship detection

**4 substrate pools** (Aquitanian, Etruscan, toponym, Eteocretan) all
**PASS** the own-LM right-tail bayesian gate on Linear A. The full
cross-LM matrix (v23) shows substrate-LM-specificity for **3/4 pools**
(Aquitanian, Etruscan, Eteocretan); the toponym pool's signal is partly
Aegean-substrate-generic rather than toponym-specific. (See
`docs/findings_summary.md` §3 and §4.6.)

### Cross-script population-level reproduction

**Spearman ρ = +1.0** between Linear A and CHIC across all 4 substrate
pools — the ranks of posterior-median gap magnitudes are byte-identical.
(See chic-v4, mg-c769, in `docs/findings.md`.) This is the strongest
positive cross-script result the framework produces.

### Per-sign decipherment unsupported under any pool

- **chic-v9** (mg-18cb) leave-one-out: aggregate accuracy 20.0% (4/20),
  tier-2 unanimity 0/3 correct.
- **v28** (mg-4a7b) LA-side LOO on LB-carryover anchors: aggregate
  accuracy 33.3% (7/21), tier-2 unanimity 0/3 correct.
- **v22** (mg-46d5) scholar comparison: 3.95% match rate.

The framework's **at-chance per-sign behaviour is structural, not
corpus-specific** — both LA-side LOO (v28) and CHIC-side LOO (chic-v9)
land in the low-agreement / not-validated band. (See §3, §4.6, §4.7.)

### Per-candidate evidence-grading within the chic-v9-validated low-accuracy band

Post-chic-v15, the chic sub-program has produced **n = 19
context-inspection-consistent candidates** total across the candidate
pool:

- `#032` from chic-v11 (mg-d69c) — paired cross-pool L3 + context
  inspection.
- 6 from chic-v13 (mg-5261) — paired cross-pool L3 + context inspection
  on chic-v12 tier-2-equivalent set.
- 12 from chic-v15 (mg-2904) — context-inspection-only on chic-v12
  tier-3-uncorroborated set.

Of these 19, **n = 7 carry paired cross-pool L3 + within-window
context-inspection evidence**: `#032`, `#021`, `#005`, `#072`, `#017`,
`#039`, `#056`. The remaining 12 are context-inspection-only.

**Cross-pool L3 alone is anti-evidentiary on the tier-3 set.** chic-v14
(mg-7f57) leave-one-out: 12/20 = 60.0% of known anchors recovered to
tier-2-equivalent under cross-pool L3 alone vs chic-v12's 8/29 = 27.6%
on the tier-3 set — a **-32.4pp deficit** below the LOO baseline.

**Cross-pool L3 has no independent discriminative value** beyond
within-window context inspection. chic-v15 (mg-2904) ran context
inspection on the 17 chic-v12 tier-3-uncorroborated candidates (where
cross-pool L3 fails completely) and got 12/17 = 70.6% pass rate,
comparable to chic-v13's 75% on tier-3-corroborated.

### Within-window context inspection is the load-bearing fourth pillar

The chic-v14 + chic-v15 pair collectively demote cross-pool L3 to **"no
independent discriminative value"** — at-best a permissive corroboration
test that adds no information beyond context inspection.

## D. Possible follow-up work (in priority order, my read)

These are **methodologically symmetric / within product scope** items
that have not been done. They are filed here as siblings if Daniel
revives the line. **Priority is mg-811e's read, not Daniel's preference**
— future-pm-lineara should re-evaluate priority on revival; Daniel's
explicit framing in section G is that goals may have changed by then.

### D.1. Linear B control test  *(my pick for cleanest single missed slice)*

Run the chic-v5 / chic-v11 / chic-v12 / chic-v13 / chic-v14
evidence-grading chain on **Linear B** treating its (already-known)
per-sign values as unknown. v28 (mg-4a7b) already ran LOO on LB-carryover
anchors at 33.3% accuracy / 0/3 tier-2 correct; the *full* chain on LB
would close the loop on v28's "structural, not CHIC-specific" claim.

**Either outcome refines the manuscript:**

- If the framework recovers known LB values at materially higher than
  LA/CHIC rates, the LA/CHIC failure is **corpus-specific** —
  interesting because LB and LA are paleographic siblings.
- If it fails on LB too, the framework is **fundamentally limited at
  per-sign decipherment regardless of corpus**.

Single ticket. **Budget estimate ~1.5M tokens.**

### D.2. Eteocretan-Greek bilingual constraint

Praisos and Dreros carry **partial Greek translations** alongside
Eteocretan inscriptions. chic-v8 (mg-dfcc) attempted bilingual constraint
as L5 evidence on the Malia altar and was null because v0 corpora didn't
carry the Greek side; the Praisos/Dreros bilinguals do.

Methodologically symmetric to chic-v8 with corpus that actually supports
the test. Could either validate or invalidate the Eteocretan-LM substrate
detection at the population level. **Budget estimate ~1.2M tokens.**

### D.3. Control-pool extension

Add **1–2 control substrate pools** (Hurrian, Hittite, Phoenician —
well-attested separate corpora) to test whether the population-level
substrate-detection signal is **Old-European-specific** or a generic
**Bronze-Age-writing-system property**. If the framework PASSes on
Hurrian too, we are picking up generic structure rather than
substrate-specific signal — that would weaken the manuscript's positive
claim. Worth knowing before any specialist review.

**Budget estimate ~1.0M tokens.**
